Company History

McDonald’s was founded in 1940 by brothers Richard and Maurice McDonald in California, USA. From the very beginning, the company focused on providing fast and tasty food that was accessible to a wide audience. In 1955, Ray Kroc, who became a key partner of the company, opened the first restaurant under the McDonald’s brand in Illinois and began to scale the business around the world.

Today, McDonald’s is one of the most famous and successful restaurant chains in the world, with more than 40,000 restaurants in 120 countries. The company continues to adhere to its founding principles – quality, speed and convenience for customers, offering a variety of menu items, including classic burgers, salads, desserts, and drinks. McDonald’s invariably remains a symbol of tasty and affordable food for millions of people around the world.
